Method notes

Weibull: loss = frequency-weighted E[P | v < threshold] from the sector wake-Weibull, multiplied by active hours. Assumes the annual wind distribution also holds during bat-active hours.

Time series: every timestep inside an NZ window with valid temperature is checked against the threshold; if wind < threshold, the power-curve value is added as loss. Captures the night/summer wind correlation. Optional per-turbine wind scale adjusts meteo wind to hub conditions.

All computation runs locally in your browser.

How to use
1

Choose a method

Weibull reproduces the windPRO-style sector-statistics calculation. Time series evaluates every 10-min step of the meteo data (needs a wind speed column). Compare both shows them side by side.

2

Load meteo data

Select the windPRO raw CSV (; or , separated), map the DateTime, temperature and wind columns, set the year range, then press Parse file. German decimal commas are handled automatically. Alternatively enter active hours manually.

3

Add turbines

Per turbine paste the 12-row sector table (sector angle freq% A k v_free v_wake) and the power curve (wind_speed power, in W or kW — detected automatically). Optionally add a windPRO AEP reference for validation and a wind scale factor for the time-series method.

4

Check configuration

NZ windows, monthly thresholds, classic scheme and the temperature filter are pre-filled with your project defaults and fully editable.

5

Calculate & export

Results show full AEP, classic loss, NZ loss and the saving per turbine, plus the full NZ matrix. Export everything as a multi-sheet .xlsx.
